Pandemic wave creates North American bottleneck

"The virus has thrown off the choreography of moving cargo from one continent to another. Americans stuck in their homes have set off a surge of orders from factories in China, much of it carried across the Pacific in containers — the metal boxes that move goods in towering stacks atop enormous vessels. As households in the United States have filled bedrooms with office furniture and basements with treadmills, the demand for shipping has outstripped the availability of containers in Asia, yielding shortages there just as the boxes pile up at American ports."|

Carriers jump on lucrative transpacific profits...

Container shipping rates have been surging on all East to West shipping routes since May, with the highest rate upturn hitting the transpacific.
Before 2020, transporting a standard 40-foot (12-meter) container on a ship sailing from a Chinese port cost about $1,000 (€840) — currently, one must pay up to $10,000.
Eastbound container freight rates have not just increased a lot since the beginning of the
COVID-19 outbreak; they have more than doubled, eclipsing all historical highs.

But many Ocean Carriers claimed when the pandemic started, many factories temporarily closed which caused many containers to be stopped at ports. Therefor to the stabilize costs and theerosion of ocean rates, carriers reduced the number of vessels out at sea.
Not only did this put the brakes on import and export, but it also meant empty containers were not picked up.
